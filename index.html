A Quiet Place Where You Still Live

The Words I Never Said

I’ve held this inside for a long time, and I’m writing not because I expect anything, but because some things deserve to be said out loud, even if they’re only for you and the quiet of this page.

You were once my whole world — not in some loud, dramatic way, but in a thousand small details that added up until I didn’t recognise life without them. I miss your stupid giggles, how they’d spill out of you at the smallest thing and make everything lighter. I miss the way your eyes would find mine — those beautiful eyes that somehow made it impossible for me to look anywhere else. When you smiled, your eyes would crease into almost a perfect “C,” and that little shape would warm me more than any words could. Your smile could heat me up in a room full of coldness; you would shine in a crowd and somehow make the rest of the world quiet so only you mattered.

Your presence lives freely in my mind. It’s not loud or constant, but it comes back in the night when everything else is still. In the silent hours you return without me asking you to, in small, unpredictable ways — a scent on a shirt, a line from a song, a face in a passing crowd. Sometimes I’ll catch myself smiling at a memory of your cute, awkward half-smile that didn’t want to show much, but was the perfect balance of beautiful and shy. Other times I’ll remember your blur face when you didn’t catch on to what I was saying and I’ll laugh at how honest you were without trying.

Echoes of What Was Ours

I loved — I still love — how you knew me before I could say the simplest things. You knew how to love me, how to treat me, in ways no one else could. Your affection was quiet and fierce at once; it didn’t need to announce itself to be felt. I love hearing you talk, and I loved the small, steady way you would nod and let me be heard. Those tiny gestures mattered more than you ever knew.

You brought an enormous impact into my life despite your size. It’s strange how something so unassuming could move a heart so deeply. You carried the kind of light that made ordinary days feel heavenly. Being with you felt right — right in a way that didn’t need proof, only presence. I still remember how I couldn’t resist you: not your looks alone, but everything — the way you breathed when you were thinking, the scent that would linger and make the world softer, the warmth that seemed to follow you.

Fragments of a Heaven Once Mine

Perhaps, we lost each other over a miscommunication — sentences that turned into a wall. I replay that night more than I should, not to blame, but because I keep searching for the exact moment things shifted. If I could undo it, I would. If I could say the right thing at the right time, I would. But I also know life rarely gives perfect do-overs, and that’s why I’m writing this now: to put my truth where it belongs, not to ask you back, only to let you know what you once were to me.

You were my dime — a rare, small thing I didn’t realise how valuable until it wasn’t in my pocket anymore. Losing you left a quiet hole I’ve been learning to live with. I don’t write this to change the past or to make you feel guilty. I write it because you should know how deeply and honestly you were loved. You should know that, for all the time we spent together, it felt heavenly — like a soft place I carried inside me long after the days ended.

"I feel your presence, even when you're not here,
I notice your essence. Your touch stayed behind, leaving marks on my heart that won't fade nor be loose screws to unbind.
I try to move on, but the memories of us pull me back, like a shadow that I can't escape til the crack of dawn. You soul intertwine with mine, I drink your words and command like wine.
Every quiet moment, reminds me of how you left a part of yourself here, covered in dust like an old instrument. I ask god for peace, but the ache of your absence still fills the empty spaces of my beliefs.
My heart feels heavy with memories, whispering of what was once reveries. I try to find comfort in knowing God knows what I feel, I seek His mercy to guide me through and heal.
But I continue to feel the traces of your love, Heaven knows I long for you, Is this what Marvin Gaye was talking of? Lingering like scars on my heart, You're the black Mona Lisa to my art."

So this is my confession and my exhale. I have kept these feelings for too long. I wanted you to know the ways you shaped my nights and my better mornings. I wanted you to know that the memories are gentle, not bitter; that the longing is steady, not demanding. I carry no expectations from you — only the hope that, somewhere, you are smiling and that life treats you kindly.

If ever you stumble across this and think of me, know that I remember you fondly and that you will always hold a small, bright space in my heart. Thank you for being the person who taught me how right it could feel to be loved.

With quiet affection,
Anas
